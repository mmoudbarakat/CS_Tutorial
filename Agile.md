## Core Principles (from the Agile Manifesto)

1. **Individuals and interactions** over processes and tools  
2. **Working software** over comprehensive documentation  
3. **Customer collaboration** over contract negotiation  
4. **Responding to change** over following a plan  

> ✨ Agile is about **flexibility, feedback, and delivering value quickly**.

---

## Agile Fundamentals

### Agile
- An iterative approach to software development and project management.
- Delivers value to customers **frequently and incrementally**.

### Iteration
- A short time-boxed period (e.g., 1–4 weeks) to develop and deliver working features.

### Increment
- The working software delivered at the end of an iteration — part of the overall product.

### Timeboxing
- Assigning a fixed period to an activity (e.g., sprint, meeting) to enhance focus and productivity.

---

## Agile Roles

### Product Owner
- Defines the **product vision**.
- Manages the **backlog** and **prioritizes work** based on business value.

### Scrum Master
- Facilitates the Scrum process.
- Removes blockers and ensures the team follows Agile principles.

### Development Team
- Cross-functional group of professionals who **design, build, and test** the product.

---

## Agile Artifacts

### Product Backlog
- Ordered list of all desired product features and fixes.
- Continuously refined by the Product Owner.

### Sprint Backlog
- A subset of the product backlog selected for the current sprint.
- Represents the team’s plan for the sprint.

### Increment
- The working software at the end of a sprint — potentially shippable.

---

##  Agile Events (Scrum)

### Sprint Planning
- Meeting to decide what the team will deliver during the sprint and how it will be done.

### Daily Standup (Daily Scrum)
- Short (15-min) team meeting to sync on progress, plans, and blockers.

### Sprint Review
- Held at the end of a sprint to show the completed work to stakeholders.

### Sprint Retrospective
- Internal team meeting to reflect on what went well, what didn’t, and how to improve.

---

## Agile Metrics (optional for tracking)

- **Velocity**: Amount of work a team completes in a sprint (measured in story points or hours).
- **Burndown Chart**: Tracks remaining work in a sprint to monitor progress.
- **Lead Time**: Time from request to delivery.
- **Cycle Time**: Time from starting to finishing work on a task.

---

## Agile Frameworks

### Scrum
- Time-boxed sprints, defined roles/events/artifacts.
- Focus on team coordination and continuous improvement.

### Kanban
- Visual board with WIP (Work In Progress) limits.
- Emphasizes continuous flow and gradual improvements.

### XP (Extreme Programming)
- Focuses on **engineering practices**: pair programming, test-driven development, frequent releases.
